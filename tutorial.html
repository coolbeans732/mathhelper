🔢 Number Sense

Decimals: A decimal is just a way to show part of a whole. For example, 0.5 means half. You line up the decimal points when adding or subtracting.

Fractions: Fractions like 3/4 mean 3 parts out of 4. You can convert them to decimals by dividing: 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75.

Percent: Percent means “out of 100.” So 25% of 80 is 0.25 × 80 = 20.

🧮 Algebra

Expressions: An expression like 3x + 2 means “three times a number plus two.” You simplify by combining like terms.

Equations: To solve 2x + 5 = 15, subtract 5 from both sides, then divide by 2. You get x = 5.

📐 Geometry

Area: Area is how much space something covers. A rectangle is length × width. A triangle is ½ × base × height.

Volume: Volume is how much space something takes up. A box is length × width × height.

📊 Statistics

Mean: Add all the numbers and divide by how many there are.

Median: Put the numbers in order and find the middle one.

Mode: The number that shows up the most.

Range: Biggest number minus smallest number.
